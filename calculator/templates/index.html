why time slows near a black hole?

Time slows down near a black hole due to...
the extremely strong gravitational field of the black hole.
According to the theory of general relativity, this is due to the gravity of the black hole which curves spacetime in a way that affects all measurements of time and space near the black hole.
At the very center of the black hole is where our understanding breaks down. Einstein's theory of gravity seems to conclude that time itself is destroyed at the center of the hole, time comes to an abrupt end there and for this reason, a black hole is sometimes described as the "Reverse of Creation".
